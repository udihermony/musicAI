# Junky

**Style:** funky jazz — dark, syncopated, tight pocket
**Tempo:** 92 BPM · **Meter:** 4/4 · **Status:** iteration 1 — drums only

## The groove concept

The name is the vibe: greasy, slightly off-kilter, deeply in the pocket.
Think early-70s Miles Davis electric period meets a funk rhythm section.
Dark harmonically, syncopated rhythmically, sparse and deliberate.

## Drum track (current)

The kick is the hook. Three ideas stacked:

1. **Anchors on 1 and 3** (velocity 115) — you always know where the bar is
2. **"a-of-1" push** (step 3) — anticipates beat 2, creates forward motion
3. **"and-of-4" push** (step 14) — drives back into beat 1 of the next bar

Snare sits on the classic backbeat (2 and 4, velocity 105), but a bed of
**ghost notes** at velocity 30 runs underneath on steps 2, 6, 9, 11, 13, 15.
The ghosts are what makes it funky — they fill the space without cluttering it.

Hi-hats on the 8th notes, with the **and-of-4 as an open hat** so the groove
breathes going into the next bar. Light 8th-note swing (30 ticks) on the "and"
positions — just enough jazz flavour without losing the funk pocket.

**Cowbell** on beats 1 and 3 (velocity 65) is the jazz seasoning.

Snare fill escalates into bars 4 and 8 (steps 10–15, velocity 50 → 110).

## Possible next moves (in order of impact)

1. **Bass line** — a Fender Rhodes-style bass or electric bass. Key centre not
   decided yet; A minor or D minor would suit the dark vibe. Consider a syncopated
   figure that locks with the kick on step 3 (the a-of-1 push).

2. **Chords / Rhodes voicings** — sparse, left-hand stabs. One or two hits per
   bar, pushing on the off-beats. Extended voicings (9ths, 11ths, 13ths).
   Don't resolve everything — keep it harmonically ambiguous.

3. **Lead / melody** — a short pentatonic or blues-scale phrase that comes in
   every 4 bars. Could be a trumpet, flute, or electric piano run.

4. **Variation on the drum pattern** — after the first 8-bar statement, vary
   the kick on bars 5–8 (e.g. add step 6 for extra syncopation, drop step 10).
   Requires a custom.py since the YAML pattern repeats identically.
